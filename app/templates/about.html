About RadPredict

Overview

RadPredict is a computational tool designed to predict the radiation resistance of bacteria through genomic analysis. This platform uses machine learning algorithms trained on genomic features to estimate D10 values, which represent the radiation dose required to reduce a bacterial population by 90%.

Developed by the Laboratory of Intelli-manufactureing Food Engineering (LIFE) at Nanjing Tech University, RadPredict aims to provide researchers and industry professionals with a rapid, reliable method to assess radiation resistance without extensive laboratory experimentation.

Scientific Principle

The prediction model in RadPredict is built upon the correlation between specific genomic features and observed radiation resistance. Our approach involves:

  1. Feature extraction - Identification of key genomic markers associated with radiation resistance
  2. Machine learning - Training of regression models on curated datasets of bacteria with known radiation resistance
  3. Validation - Testing against experimental data to ensure prediction accuracy

The predictive model primarily focuses on:

  • DNA repair mechanisms
  • Antioxidant systems
  • Cell wall characteristics
  • Membrane composition
  • Protein protection mechanisms
Technical Details

RadPredict employs a two-stage machine learning approach:

Feature Selection

Genetic features are selected based on their correlation with radiation resistance in known organisms. This includes specific genes, protein domains, and regulatory elements.

Regression Model

A gradient boosting regression model trained on a diverse dataset of bacteria with experimentally determined D10 values predicts radiation resistance of new samples.

Usage Guide

Input Requirements

RadPredict accepts the following genomic data formats:

  • FASTA (.fasta, .fa) - Genomic sequence files
  • FNA (.fna) - Nucleotide FASTA files
  • FAA (.faa) - Protein FASTA files (optional, to skip the annotation step)
Step-by-Step Process
1. Upload

Upload your bacterial genome files through the home page. Multiple files can be processed in a single analysis.

2. Process

The system will automatically extract features, annotate genes (if necessary), and run the prediction model.

3. Results

View the predicted D10 values and download comprehensive results for further analysis.

Tip: For best results, use complete genome sequences rather than draft genomes or partial sequences.
